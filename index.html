The Future is Female

Women have made incredible strides in various fields and have proven their mettle time and time again. From leading nations to leading corporations, women have shattered glass ceilings and overcome obstacles to achieve greatness. Their perseverance and dedication have inspired countless others to follow in their footsteps, and their contributions have changed the course of history. Whether it is in science, politics, or the arts, women have demonstrated their skills and capabilities with excellence. We should continue to celebrate and support women for their accomplishments and recognize the value they bring to the world. We owe a debt of gratitude to the trailblazers who have paved the way for future generations of women, and we must continue to empower and uplift women so that they can achieve even greater heights.

First Indian Women

India has many firsts when it comes to women's achievements. Here are some notable firsts in India in women's history:
→ Sarla Thakral was the first Indian woman to fly an aircraft in 1936.
→ Rajkumari Amrit Kaur was the first woman to hold a cabinet post in India as the Minister of Health in 1957.
→ Bachendri Pal became the first Indian woman to climb Mount Everest in 1984.
→ Kalpana Chawla was the first Indian-born woman to go to space in 1997.
→ Kiran Bedi became the first woman to join the Indian Police Service in 1972.
→ Mithali Raj holds the record for being the highest run-scorer in women's international cricket.
→ Sania Mirza was the first Indian woman to win a Grand Slam title in tennis in 2009.
→ Indira Gandhi was the first woman to be elected as the Prime Minister of India in 1966.

→ Dipa Karmakar became the first Indian female gymnast to qualify for the Olympics in 52 years. She finished fourth in the vault event at the 2016 Rio Olympics.

→ Mithali Raj is the highest run-scorer in women's international cricket and the first woman to score 7,000 runs in ODIs. She has also led the Indian women's cricket team to many victories.

→ Saina Nehwal was the first Indian woman to reach the top ranking in badminton. She won a bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics.

→ Sania Mirza is a former world No. 1 in women's doubles and has won several Grand Slam titles. She has also won six medals, including three golds, at the Asian Games.

→ Karnam Malleswari was the first Indian woman to win a medal at the Olympics. She won a bronze medal in weightlifting at the 2000 Sydney Olympics.

→ Saikhom Mirabai Chanu scripted history by winning the Silver Medal in the Women’s 49 kg Weightlifting event at the Tokyo Olympics in 2021. Mirabai Chanu lifted a total of 202 kilograms.

→ Lovlina Borgohain at the Tokyo Olympics 2020, took home the Bronze medal in the women’s 69kg boxing event. With this victory, Lovlina Borgohain became the third Indian boxer to earn a medal at the Olympics. After M.C Mary Kom, she is the second Indian female boxer to win an Olympic medal.

→ Jyothi Surekha Vennam set a new record at the World Archery Championship, 2021.

→ Avani Lekhara made history at the Tokyo Paralympics 2020. She is now the first Indian woman to win multiple medals at the Paralympic Games.

→ Bhavinaben Patel won a historic silver medal at the Tokyo Paralympics 2020 bagging the first ever silver in table tennis for India. She won silver in the women’s singles Class 4 table tennis event.

...

→ Nandini Harinath is an Indian rocket scientist who has been associated with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) for over two decades. She has worked on several missions, including the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), which made India the first Asian country to successfully reach Mars.

→ Minal Rohit is an Indian scientist who worked on the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) as a part of the team that developed the spacecraft's propulsion system.

→ Anuradha TK is a scientist and director at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). She has played a key role in the development of several satellites, including the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) and the Chandrayaan-2 mission.

→ Sudha Bhattacharya is an Indian space scientist who was a part of the team that developed India's first satellite, Aryabhata. She has also contributed to the development of several other satellites and space missions.

→ Vanitha Muthayya is an Indian engineer who served as the project director for the Chandrayaan-2 mission. She played a key role in the development and launch of India's second mission to the moon.

Measure

Empowering women is critical to achieving gender equality, social justice, and sustainable development. Here are some measures that can be taken to ensure women's empowerment:
→ Education: Provide equal access to education for girls and women to ensure they have the knowledge and skills to participate fully in society and the economy.
→ Health and reproductive rights: Ensure women have access to quality healthcare and reproductive rights, including family planning, maternal health services, and information on sexual and reproductive health.
→ Economic empowerment: Create opportunities for women to participate in the workforce, own businesses, and have access to financial resources and markets.
→ Political participation: Encourage and support women's participation in political processes, including voting, running for office, and decision-making positions.
→ Eliminating gender-based violence: Address the root causes of gender-based violence and take steps to prevent and respond to all forms of violence against women.
→ Cultural and social change: Work towards cultural and social change by challenging gender stereotypes, promoting positive role models for women, and raising awareness about gender equality.
→ Legal reform: Enact and enforce laws and policies that protect women's rights, including equal pay, non-discrimination, and access to justice.
→ Mentoring and leadership development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities for women to build their skills and confidence and advance in their careers and communities.
→ Media representation: Promote positive images of women in the media and increase women's representation in all forms of media, including news, entertainment, and advertising.

Overall, to ensure women empowerment, a comprehensive approach is needed that addresses the various factors that contribute to gender inequality and supports women's access to education, employment, healthcare, legal and political support, and financial resources.
